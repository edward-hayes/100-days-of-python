- Two main font families (Serif, Sans-Serif)

Serif:
- have little "feet" at the end of the central beams
- based on marble fonts
- more serious, older, authorative
- good for letterhead

    Subfonts:
    Old Style
    Traditional
    Modern

Sans-Serif
- No feet, has right angles
- very readable
- good for body text


Emotion behind fonts:
SERIF = Traditional, Stable, Respectable
SANS-SERIF = Sensible, Simple, Straightforward
SCRIPT = Personal, Creative, Elegant
DISPLAY = Friendly, Loud, Amusing
MODERN = Stylish, Chic, Smart

General keep to two fonts on one website. 
Keep Mood and Time Era same
Contrast Serif-ness & Weights
